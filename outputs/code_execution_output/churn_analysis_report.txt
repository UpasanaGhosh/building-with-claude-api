
================================================================================
                    CHURN ANALYSIS - EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
                          Streaming Service Dataset
================================================================================

DATASET OVERVIEW:
-----------------
• Total Customers Analyzed: 500
• Churned Customers: 193 (38.6%)
• Retained Customers: 307 (61.4%)

================================================================================
                         MAJOR DRIVERS OF CHURN
================================================================================

RANK 1: VIEWING ENGAGEMENT (Primary Driver - 60% of predictive power)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
The single most important cluster of factors driving churn relates to user 
engagement with the platform:

A. Total Viewing Hours (Importance: 23.8%)
   • Churned customers: 66.6 hours/month
   • Retained customers: 83.2 hours/month
   • Impact: -20.0% lower engagement among churned users
   • Interpretation: Low viewing hours is the strongest predictor of churn

B. Average Session Duration (Importance: 21.5%)
   • Churned customers: 49.4 minutes per session
   • Retained customers: 57.8 minutes per session
   • Impact: -14.4% shorter sessions among churned users
   • Interpretation: Users who don't engage deeply in content are at risk

C. Number of Unique Titles Watched (Importance: 15.7%)
   • Churned customers: 19.5 titles/month
   • Retained customers: 23.7 titles/month
   • Impact: -18.1% less content variety among churned users
   • Interpretation: Limited content exploration indicates lower satisfaction

D. Binge Watching Sessions (Importance: 11.0%)
   • Churned customers: 6.2 sessions/month
   • Retained customers: 7.7 sessions/month
   • Impact: -19.7% fewer binge sessions among churned users
   • Interpretation: Binge-watching behavior indicates strong engagement

KEY INSIGHT: All engagement metrics show churned customers are significantly
less engaged. This suggests that monitoring engagement metrics can provide
early warning signals for potential churn.

================================================================================

RANK 2: CUSTOMER SERVICE INTERACTIONS (Importance: 9.7%)
---------------------------------------------------------
• Churned customers: 3.18 interactions/year
• Retained customers: 2.49 interactions/year
• Impact: +27.7% more service interactions among churned users
• Correlation with churn: +0.279 (strongest positive correlation)

INTERPRETATION: High customer service interactions are a red flag. This 
suggests that users who experience problems (technical issues, billing issues,
content dissatisfaction) are more likely to churn. The service interactions
may be symptoms of underlying problems rather than the cause itself.

================================================================================

RANK 3: CONTENT PREFERENCES (Importance: 11.1%)
------------------------------------------------
Churn rates vary significantly by content preference:

HIGH CHURN GENRES:
• Horror: 52.3% churn rate (23 of 44 customers)
• Thriller: 48.3% churn rate (14 of 29 customers)
• Action: 44.6% churn rate (33 of 74 customers)

LOW CHURN GENRES:
• Documentary: 25.9% churn rate (14 of 54 customers)
• Comedy: 33.0% churn rate (33 of 100 customers)
• Drama: 35.3% churn rate (36 of 102 customers)

INTERPRETATION: Customers whose top genre is Horror or Thriller churn at 
twice the rate of Documentary viewers. This may indicate:
1. Insufficient content depth in these genres
2. Different engagement patterns (e.g., seasonal viewing)
3. These genres may attract more casual viewers

================================================================================

RANK 4: SUBSCRIPTION TIER (Importance: 3.3%)
---------------------------------------------
Churn rates by tier:
• Basic Tier ($7.99/mo): 43.5% churn - HIGHEST RISK
  - 207 customers, 90 churned
• Standard Tier ($12.99/mo): 39.5% churn - MODERATE RISK
  - 210 customers, 83 churned
• Premium Tier ($17.99/mo): 24.1% churn - LOWEST RISK
  - 83 customers, 20 churned

INTERPRETATION: Premium subscribers churn at half the rate of Basic 
subscribers. This could indicate:
1. Users who value the service upgrade and stay
2. Basic tier may lack perceived value
3. Premium features drive stronger retention

================================================================================

RANK 5: MONTHLY COST (Importance: 3.9%)
----------------------------------------
• Churned customers: $11.18 average monthly cost
• Retained customers: $12.11 average monthly cost
• Impact: -7.7% lower cost among churned users

INTERPRETATION: Paradoxically, churned customers pay slightly less on average.
This aligns with the subscription tier finding - cheaper plans have higher 
churn. This suggests it's not about price sensitivity, but rather about the
value proposition and features offered at each tier.

================================================================================
                          ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATIONS
================================================================================

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (High Impact, Quick Implementation):
-------------------------------------------------------
1. IMPLEMENT ENGAGEMENT MONITORING SYSTEM
   • Set up alerts for customers below engagement thresholds:
     - < 70 hours viewing/month
     - < 50 minutes average session duration
     - < 20 unique titles watched/month
   • Trigger proactive retention campaigns for at-risk users

2. INVESTIGATE CUSTOMER SERVICE ROOT CAUSES
   • Analyze support tickets to identify common issues
   • Address systemic problems causing repeated contacts
   • Implement preventive measures to reduce need for support

3. ENHANCE BASIC TIER VALUE PROPOSITION
   • Review what drives users to Premium tier
   • Consider adding select features to Basic tier
   • Test messaging around value at the Basic tier price point

SHORT-TERM INITIATIVES (1-3 months):
------------------------------------
4. CONTENT INVESTMENT BY GENRE
   • Increase Horror and Thriller content library depth
   • Monitor genre-specific engagement metrics
   • Consider genre-specific recommendation improvements

5. PERSONALIZED RE-ENGAGEMENT CAMPAIGNS
   • Target low-engagement users with personalized recommendations
   • Send curated content suggestions based on viewing history
   • Implement "new content in your favorite genre" notifications

6. EARLY WARNING SYSTEM
   • Build predictive model using identified drivers
   • Score all active customers on churn risk
   • Create tiered intervention strategies by risk level

LONG-TERM STRATEGIC INITIATIVES (3-6 months):
----------------------------------------------
7. PRICING AND TIER OPTIMIZATION
   • Test different tier configurations
   • Consider feature bundling to drive upgrades
   • Evaluate graduated pricing based on usage

8. CONTENT STRATEGY REFINEMENT
   • Deep dive into genre-specific viewing patterns
   • Invest strategically in content for high-churn genres
   • Consider genre-specific subscription options

9. PROACTIVE ENGAGEMENT PROGRAMS
   • Gamification to encourage content exploration
   • Social features to increase stickiness
   • Viewing challenges/rewards programs
